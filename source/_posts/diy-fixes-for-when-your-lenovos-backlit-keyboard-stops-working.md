## How to arrange your desktop icons

 It takes you only a few steps to arrange the desktop icons. Here’s how to do it:

1) Right-click a blank area on your desktop to open the context menu.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-376.png)

 2) Click**Sort by** and choose one command (either Name, Size, Item type, or Date modified) from the right menu. These commands indicate how you want to arrange the icons.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-379.png)

 3) Now your desktop icons should be displayed in an orderly way. Besides, if you don’t like to arrange the icons manually, you may click**View > Auto arrange icons** and let Windows do the configuration for you.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-381.png)

 These are the simple and useful steps that help you arrange the desktop icons. If you want to lock them in place, please move on to the next part.

---

## How to stop desktop icons from moving randomly

 It is most annoying when you find your desktop icons keep moving all the time. You just want them to stay in the place where they should be, rather than wander here and there like a rover. If that’s the case, you should go on with the steps below to lock your desktop icons.

 1) Right-click an empty area on your desktop to open the context menu. Click**View** and then**Align icons to grid** . Make sure the**Auto arrange icons** option is**unticked** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-378.png)

 2) Click**Personalize** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-383.png)

 3) In the pop-up window, select**Themes** from its left pane. Then click**Desktop icon settings** on the right panel.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-384.png)

 4) Make sure you uncheck the**Allow themes to change desktop icons** option. Then click**OK** to save the change.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/image-385.png)

 5) Run a full scan for viruses and malware with your antivirus program to ensure that your PC has not been infected.

 6) With all the steps above completed, you should now check to see whether the icons are still moving randomly.
